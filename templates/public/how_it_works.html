{% translate "How it works" %}

{% translate "What is the 4 Vallées Ambassadors Program?" %}

{% blocktranslate trimmed %} The 4 Vallées Ambassadors Program is a referral scheme that lets a returning season-pass holder (an Ambassador) bring a genuinely new holder (a Referee) into the programme. Both apply together and, if eligible, both receive a discount on their seasonal pass. {% endblocktranslate %}

{% translate "Who is an Ambassador and who is a Referee?" %}

{% blocktranslate trimmed %} An Ambassador is someone who held a 4 Vallées seasonal or annual pass (or a Mont 4 Card) in a prior season. A Referee is someone who has never held such a pass — they are genuinely new to the 4 Vallées programme. Each Ambassador sponsors exactly one Referee. {% endblocktranslate %}

{% translate "How do I apply?" %}

{% blocktranslate trimmed %} Download the application form, fill it in together with your partner, and email it to customer@televerbier.ch. You and your partner must sign the form and both be present at the ticket office to purchase your passes together. {% endblocktranslate %}

{% translate "Download application form" %}

{% translate "What is the process?" %}

{% blocktranslate trimmed %} Once matched, both the Ambassador and the Referee fill in the application form together and email it to customer@televerbier.ch. They then visit the ticket office together to sign the form in person and purchase their passes at the kiosk. {% endblocktranslate %}

{% translate "What are the requirements?" %}

{% blocktranslate trimmed %} Both the Ambassador and the Referee must sign the application form and provide a valid passport or national identity card at the ticket office. The Referee must not have held a 4 Vallées seasonal or annual pass in any prior season — this is self-attested on the form and verified at the kiosk. {% endblocktranslate %}

{% translate "What does this site do — and not do?" %}

{% blocktranslate trimmed %} This site handles only the matchmaking: it connects an Ambassador with a Referee so they can apply together. It does not collect the full application details (date of birth, address, photo ID, keycard, or insurance) and it is not connected to the ticket-office purchase system. Filling in the form and buying at the kiosk happen off-app and are unchanged. {% endblocktranslate %}

{% translate "How does the match work?" %}

{% blocktranslate trimmed %} When you register, the system places you in a queue and pairs you with an eligible partner as soon as one becomes available. You do not browse or choose — the system assigns a match. Once proposed, both parties have a fixed window to accept. Neither side sees the other's contact details until both have accepted. {% endblocktranslate %}

{% translate "What happens next?" %}

{% blocktranslate trimmed %} Once both parties accept the match, each receives the other's contact details. They then arrange to fill in the application form and visit the ticket office together. If either party declines or the acceptance window lapses, both re-enter the queue and the system proposes a new match. {% endblocktranslate %}
